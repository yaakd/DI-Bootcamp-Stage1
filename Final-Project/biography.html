Biography

1947: Birth

Arnold Schwarzenegger was born on July 30, 1947, in Thal, Styria, Austria, to Aurelia and Gustav Schwarzenegger.

1965: Bodybuilding Beginnings

At the age of 18, Arnold began his bodybuilding career, winning the Junior Mr. Europe contest in 1965.

1968: Move to the United States

Arnold moved to the United States at the age of 21 to pursue his bodybuilding career and acting dreams.

1969: First Acting Role

Arnold made his acting debut in the film "Hercules in New York," where he was credited as "Arnold Strong."

1970: First Mr. Olympia Title

Arnold won his first Mr. Olympia title in 1970 at the age of 23, making him the youngest Mr. Olympia winner at the time. He would go on to win the title for six consecutive years until 1975 and claim his seventh title in 1980.

1977: "Pumping Iron" Documentary

The documentary "Pumping Iron" was released in 1977, showcasing the world of professional bodybuilding and featuring Arnold Schwarzenegger as a prominent figure in the sport.

1982: "Conan the Barbarian" Breakthrough Role

Arnold's breakthrough acting role came in 1982 with the release of "Conan the Barbarian," which led to a successful acting career.





1984: "The Terminator" Franchise Begins

Arnold starred as the iconic Terminator in the 1984 film directed by James Cameron. The film was a massive success, leading to multiple sequels and solidifying Arnold's status as a leading action star.

1986: Marriage to Maria Shriver

Arnold married Maria Shriver, a journalist and member of the Kennedy family, in 1986. The couple has four children together. They separated in 2011.

1990-2002: Continued Acting Success

Throughout the 1990s and early 2000s, Arnold continued to star in successful films such as "Total Recall," "Kindergarten Cop," "True Lies," and "Terminator 2: Judgment Day."

2003: Elected Governor of California

Arnold was elected as the 38th Governor of California in 2003, serving two terms until 2011. He was a Republican governor in a predominantly Democratic state and focused on environmental and fiscal issues during his tenure.

2011-Present: Return to Acting and Philanthropy

Following his time as governor, Arnold returned to acting with roles in films such as "The Expendables" series, "Terminator Genisys," and "Terminator: Dark Fate." He has also been actively involved in various philanthropic endeavors, including environmental causes, after-school programs, and the Special Olympics.
